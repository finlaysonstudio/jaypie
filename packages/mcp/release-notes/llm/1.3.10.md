## Changes

- New `effort` option on `LlmOperateOptions` — a provider-neutral reasoning
  control, a five-point relative scale where only the anchored `medium` borrows
  a provider word: `lowest | low | medium | high | highest` (exported as
  `LLM.EFFORT`, type `LlmEffort`).
- Each adapter translates `effort` to its provider's native control, spreading
  the scale across that provider's available range, so one value is safe across
  providers and fallback chains. Omitting `effort` leaves the provider default
  untouched.

  | `effort`  | OpenAI `reasoning.effort` | Anthropic `output_config.effort` | Gemini 3 `thinkingLevel` | Gemini 2.5 `thinkingBudget` | Grok `reasoning_effort` | OpenRouter `reasoning.effort` |
  |-----------|---------|--------|---------|-------|--------|---------|
  | lowest    | minimal | low    | MINIMAL | 512   | low    | minimal |
  | low       | low     | low    | LOW     | 4096  | low    | low     |
  | medium    | medium  | medium | MEDIUM  | 8192  | medium | medium  |
  | high      | high    | high   | HIGH    | 16384 | high   | high    |
  | highest   | xhigh   | max    | HIGH    | 24576 | high   | xhigh   |

- When a neutral level has no distinct native rung and collapses/clamps onto a
  neighbor (e.g. `highest` → Grok `high`), the adapter emits a `log.debug` for
  the record.
- Gating (effort is ignored, never errors, where reasoning is unavailable):
  - **OpenAI** — reasoning models only (`gpt-5+`, `o`-series); merged with the
    auto `reasoning.summary`. Extremes are version-gated: `xhigh` (`highest`)
    for gpt-5.2+ and `minimal` (`lowest`) for gpt-5.4+ (non-monotonic history —
    present on 5/5.1, dropped at 5.2, back on the current line); older models
    clamp the end to `high`/`low`.
  - **Anthropic** — Claude 4.5+ / 5 models only; merged alongside a
    structured-output `format` config.
  - **Google** — `thinkingLevel` for Gemini 3.x, `thinkingBudget` for 2.5.
  - **xAI** — models whose name advertises reasoning (e.g.
    `grok-4-1-fast-reasoning`); bare `grok-latest` and `*-non-reasoning`
    skipped.
  - **OpenRouter** — always forwarded; maps to the routed model's nearest level.
  - **Bedrock** — not yet wired; `effort` is ignored.
- First-class `effort` takes precedence over a raw `providerOptions.reasoning`,
  matching the `temperature` convention. `providerOptions` remains the escape
  hatch for finer control (specific token budgets, `minimal`/`none`).

## Model Constants

- New **`PROVIDER.<name>.DEFAULT`** — a single default model per provider,
  replacing reliance on the size-tier map. Default ids are modernized:
  OpenAI `gpt-5.6-sol`, Anthropic `claude-sonnet-5`, Google `gemini-3.5-flash`,
  OpenRouter `anthropic/claude-sonnet-5`, Bedrock `amazon.nova-pro-v1:0`
  (xAI `grok-latest` unchanged).
- New **`MODEL.OPENROUTER`** subtree of provider-prefixed routes: `GLM`
  (`z-ai/glm-5.2`), `LUNA` (`openai/gpt-5.6-luna`), `SONNET`
  (`anthropic/claude-sonnet-5`).
- **Deprecated** (retired in 2.0): the per-provider size-tier map
  `PROVIDER.<name>.MODEL.{DEFAULT,LARGE,SMALL,TINY}`, the `DEFAULT.MODEL`
  bundle, and `ALL`. Use `PROVIDER.*.DEFAULT` for defaults and `MODEL.*` for
  named models. `determineModelProvider` no longer consults the tier map;
  detection relies on `MODEL_MATCH_WORDS` and the `"/"` OpenRouter rule.

## Migration

- `effort` is opt-in and additive — no changes required.
- **Default models moved.** A call with no `model` (or a bare provider name)
  now resolves to the modernized `PROVIDER.*.DEFAULT` above — notably the base
  OpenAI default is `gpt-5.6-sol` (was `gpt-5.4`) and Anthropic is
  `claude-sonnet-5` (was `claude-sonnet-4-6`). Pin an explicit `model` if you
  relied on a previous default.
- `PROVIDER.*.MODEL.*` and `ALL` still work but emit deprecation warnings;
  migrate to `PROVIDER.*.DEFAULT` / `MODEL.*`.
